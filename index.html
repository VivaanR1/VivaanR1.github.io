The Basic Fundamentals of Supply and Demand

What is Demand?

Demand refers to the quantity of products or services that a consumer in a market wants to buy at a certain price. The law of demand emphasizes that as the price of a product increases, the demand for the respective product decreases; vice versa. The quantity of the goods or products that consumers want to buy depends on the price of the good.

Related Information

  • An inferior good refers to a good whose demand decreases as the consumers' income increases while a normal good refers to a good whose demand increases as the consumers' income increases
  • Substitute goods are interchangeable goods due to the fact that as the price of a good increases, the demand for its substitute good increases.
  • Demand is affected by the number of consumers in a market and the overall income of the consumers

What is Supply?

Supply refers to the various quantities of goods/products that a seller in a market wants to produce. The law of supply emphasizes that the supply of a good increases as its price increases. A seller sells a certain quantity or amount of a product/service at a specific price.

Related Information

  • Supply is affected by the number of sellers in a market, subsidies, and government regulation
